The Bigger Picture

Your T2 Return Should Be a Strategic Asset, Not a Compliance Chore

For most Canadian corporations, the T2 return is the single most important tax document filed each year — yet it's routinely treated as a box to check rather than an opportunity to optimize. The T2 encompasses dozens of schedules covering capital cost allowances, associated corporation relationships, shareholder loans, loss carryforwards, and much more. Each schedule is an opportunity to minimize tax; each error or omission is a potential CRA reassessment waiting to happen.

Key Metrics Accounting's corporate tax team brings deep CCPC expertise to every T2 engagement. We don't just prepare your return — we review your corporate structure, identify every eligible deduction, claim every available credit, and document every position to withstand CRA scrutiny. The result is a return that minimizes your tax bill while being completely bulletproof — filed accurately and on time, every single year.

  • CCPC small business deduction (15%)
  • SR&ED research and development credits

Common Questions

Corporate Tax FAQs

Everything Canadian business owners most frequently ask about T2 filings and corporate tax obligations.

Your T2 is due 6 months after your fiscal year-end. Tax owing is due 2 months after your year-end (3 months for CCPCs paying at the 15% small business rate). We track all deadlines and alert you well in advance — usually 90 days before filing is due — ensuring you're never caught off guard.

Yes — all active Canadian corporations must file a T2 annually, even with zero income, until formally dissolved. Failing to file triggers CRA penalties regardless of tax owed. Nil returns are straightforward to prepare, but missing them creates a compliance record problem that can complicate future dealings with the CRA.

The CCPC small business deduction (SBD) reduces the federal corporate tax rate from 15% to 9% on the first $500,000 of active business income. Combined with provincial rates, this can mean an effective tax rate as low as 12.2% (depending on province), saving up to $30,000 per year compared to the general corporate rate. Maintaining CCPC eligibility and maximizing the SBD limit is one of our core priorities for every eligible corporation.
