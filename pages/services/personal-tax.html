The Opportunity

Your T1 Return Isn't Just a Form — It's a Financial Opportunity

The T1 General return touches nearly every aspect of your financial life — employment, investments, real estate, business income, family, retirement, and more. Yet the majority of Canadians who self-file their returns leave significant money on the table every single year. Not through fraud or aggressive interpretation, but simply because the tax code is dense, deduction rules are nuanced, and most people don't know what they don't know. A CPA who reviews 60+ deduction categories systematically will almost always find something that a self-filer missed.

Our personal tax team brings the same precision and depth to individual returns that we apply to complex corporate structures. Whether you have a straightforward T4 with a few RRSP contributions, or a multi-layered situation involving rental properties, self-employment, foreign investments, and life events — we handle it all with equal thoroughness and a relentless focus on maximizing every dollar you're legally entitled to claim.

  • T1 General return preparation
  • RRSP contribution optimization
  • Investment income and capital gains
  • Rental property income (T776)
  • Self-employment income (T2125)
  • Medical, charitable, and moving expenses

Income Types We Handle

Every Income Source. Every Eligible Deduction.

Our T1 service covers every income type and life situation — no matter how complex your return, we have the expertise to handle it accurately and comprehensively.

💼

Employment Income

T4 slips, employment expenses (T2200), commission income, union dues, and professional membership fees. We verify every employer-reported figure and claim every eligible employment deduction — including home office costs if applicable.

📈

Investment Income

Dividends, interest, capital gains and losses, and foreign income (T1135 for foreign assets over $100K). We optimize the timing and reporting of your investment income to minimize tax while maintaining full CRA compliance.

🏠

Rental Properties

Rental income reporting (T776), eligible expense deductions, CCA on rental property, and principal residence exemption planning. We ensure your real estate income is reported accurately and at the lowest legal tax cost.

🏃

Self-Employment

Business income (T2125), home office deduction, vehicle expenses, and business-use asset depreciation. Whether you're a freelancer, consultant, or sole proprietor, we capture every legitimate business expense you're entitled to claim.

🎓

Life Events

Moving expenses, tuition and education credits, childcare expenses, disability tax credit (DTC), adoption expenses, and first home buyer credits. Major life transitions often come with significant tax implications — and significant opportunities.

👴

Retirement Planning

RRSP and RRIF reporting, pension income splitting, OAS/CPP optimization, age amount credits, and estate planning considerations. We help retirees and near-retirees minimize tax on fixed income streams through careful, strategic reporting.

How It Works

Effortless From Your End. Exhaustive From Ours.

We've designed our personal tax process to take as little of your time as possible — while leaving nothing to chance on our side.

01

Simple Intake

Upload your documents to our secure portal — T4s, investment slips, RRSP receipts, rental records, or whatever applies to your situation. Our detailed intake checklist guides you through exactly what to gather, and our team follows up on anything missing.

02

Expert Review

A dedicated CPA reviews every income source and systematically checks 60+ deduction and credit categories against your situation. We also compare your current return to prior years to identify any carry-forward amounts, missed elections, or multi-year planning opportunities.

03

Before Filing

We walk you through your completed return and explain exactly what we found — the deductions claimed, the credits applied, and the refund or balance owing. No surprises, no jargon. You approve the return before anything is submitted to the CRA.

04

Filed & Confirmed

Electronic NETFILE submission and delivery of your Notice of Assessment once received. We review the NOA for accuracy, flag any CRA adjustments, and keep your complete filing history on file for future reference and any CRA inquiries.

Personal Tax FAQs

The most common questions we receive from individuals about T1 filing and personal tax in Canada.

April 30 for most Canadians. Self-employed individuals (and their spouses) have until June 15 to file, but any tax owing is still due April 30 — interest starts accumulating the following day on any unpaid balance. We help you calculate your tax payable in advance and plan your payment to avoid unnecessary interest charges.

Yes — filing with no income still entitles you to GST/HST credits, Canada Child Benefit (if applicable), and establishes your RRSP contribution room for future years. It also starts the statute of limitations clock, after which the CRA generally cannot reassess. There is no downside to filing and several meaningful benefits, even with zero income to report.
