Our Approach

Wealth Management Is More Than Investments — It's a Complete Financial System

True wealth management is the disciplined integration of tax strategy, investment structure, risk management, and estate planning into a single, coherent plan aligned with your goals. Most Canadians accumulate wealth in fragments — an RRSP here, a non-registered account there, an insurance policy their advisor sold them — but no one has ever drawn it all together into a unified strategy. The result is missed optimization, unnecessary tax drag, and wealth that erodes silently every year.

At Key Metrics Accounting, we eliminate that fragmentation. As CPAs, we approach wealth planning with a tax-first lens that investment advisors and financial planners simply cannot replicate. We identify the strategies — registered account sequencing, family income splitting, corporate investment structuring, and estate coordination — that create compounding advantages over time. Our clients don't just grow wealth; they keep it, protect it, and transfer it efficiently to the next generation.

How It Works

Your Wealth Planning Journey

A structured engagement designed to move you from financial fragmentation to a complete, optimized plan.

01

Discovery Meeting

A deep, comprehensive intake of your current financial picture — all assets, liabilities, income sources, existing accounts, insurance policies, goals, timeline, and risk tolerance. No financial rock is left unturned.

02

Plan Development

A comprehensive written financial plan with financial projections across multiple scenarios, prioritized action items, tax savings quantification, and a clear implementation roadmap presented in a formal plan document.

03

Implementation

Coordinated execution of all plan elements — registered account restructuring, insurance changes, trust creation, investment reallocation, and structural adjustments — managed as a single integrated process.

04

Annual Review

A scheduled yearly plan update to reflect life changes (marriage, children, business sale), new tax laws, updated projections, and progress toward your defined financial goals — keeping your strategy current and effective.
